(Paywalled NYT article)

> "Our technology is increasingly treating the territory between the query and the answer as dead space to be eliminated, when that territory is where most of the learning actually happens."
<!--sep--> 
> "When an A.I. summary answers your search query in three seconds, the window closes before curiosity can deepen. You get what you came for, but you also lose what would have turned curiosity into learning: the adjacent article you might have read, the resulting tangent you might have followed, the connection between two ideas with no obvious relationship."
<!--sep--> 
That last part reminds me of what we lost when people stopped browsing/exploring the web on their own and relied on algorithms to feed them their content.
