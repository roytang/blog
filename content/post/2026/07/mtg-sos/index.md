This is my set recap for the **Secrets of Strixhaven** MTG set!

<!--more-->

### SOS Limited Summary / Notes

- Strixhaven was really good to me; my Arena win rate was the highest it's been since Wilds of Eldraine way back in 2023. I had a slow start, but did well in Plat and Diamond. 
- I did end up playing a bit less events than I usually do for a big premier set. I mostly stopped playing after the first quick draft period because my win rate was good enough that I ended the QD period in Diamond 3 and decided to try to push for mythic limited for the first time, and that meant playing the flashback quick draft format instead (Edge of Eternity, more on that below).
- The limited format was very synergistic, you had to make sure your cards worked well together. Officially there are only 5 draft archetypes, but 4/5-color converge was also very viable and often the strongest option. The archetypes and mechanics were pretty good.
- I generally favored the two blue archetypes, which was good because they formed a good base for splashing into the converge cards later on. My blue win rate was great!

### Paper Sealed at All Aboard Expo

On May the 29th I attended the All Aboard boardgame expo to play in a sealed SOS event. I think this was the first "big" tournament I've played since before the pandemic. There were 64 players, which meant 6 rounds. 

I had {{% card %}}Colorstorm Stallion{{% /card %}} and wanted to play UR but blue was a bit shallow so I ended up going WR:

{{% photos sos-sealed %}}

My sealed pool wasn't great; I went 4-2 which wasn't good enough for prizes, but I was still quite happy to know I'm still able to play a daylong tournament and put up a decent finish. And that a lot of the regular grinders are still doing their thing and some even still recognized me and I was able to chat with them about the good old days.
