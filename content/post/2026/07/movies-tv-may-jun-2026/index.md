These are the movies and TV shows I watched over the past couple of months.

A note about ratings: I had a convo with someone recently about "ratings inflation" where people rarely rate anything 5/10 or lower. I tend to rate everything a 2.5-3 or better as long as it didn't feel like a waste of time. For me, a midpoint rating implies that something is watchable and I don't mind seeing it again but anything below that is just something I actively disliked or even hated and would maybe rather not think about again ever.

### Movies

#### We Can Be Heroes (2020)

Watched on 2026-05-17: ★★½



{{% photos we-can-be-heroes-2020 %}}

 Only watched this one because the pamangkins were in the house. It's kind of okay for a kids movie, but still totally goofy and so so many plot holes. Pedro Pascal and Christian Slater are here!



[Review on Letterboxd](https://letterboxd.com/hungryroy/film/we-can-be-heroes-2020/)

#### Send Help (2026)

Watched on 2026-05-20: ★★★★★



{{% photos send-help-2026 %}}

 This was so good. The script was diabolically clever and just pulls you in with the tension and multiple times went in directions I wasn't expecting. Rachel McAdams is a fantastic shapeshifter, and Dylan O'Brien is great as her sniveling misogynistic nepo baby of a boss was great too. I could not have predicted how this would go!



[Review on Letterboxd](https://letterboxd.com/hungryroy/film/send-help/)

---

#### The Running Man (2025)

Watched on 2026-05-23: ★★★★



{{% photos the-running-man-2025 %}}

 I haven't seen the Arnold original. I mostly enjoyed this one, though I'm neither here nor there on Glenn Powell's acting. This one was very action-packed and unrelenting so it helps you not think too much about the plot. I was kind of expecting something a bit more insane/clever from Edgar Wright though. I'm not sure their world/economy holds up if you look at it closely, and the dystopian inequality is uncomfortably too close to our modern world.



[Review on Letterboxd](https://letterboxd.com/hungryroy/film/the-running-man-2025/)

#### Jurassic World Rebirth (2025)

Watched on 2026-06-23: ★★★½



{{% photos jurassic-world-rebirth-2025 %}}

 Apparently I skipped the last Chris Pratt one, which does not seem like a big loss.

I had low expectations for this one, but it's fine, perfectly watchable and a lot of tension where it was needed. ScarJo is a decent lead. I liked that this one wasn't anywhere near a park and mostly humans going where they know they shouldn't go. The usual corporations being greedy and getting what comes to them. The segment on the open sea was nice, not sure if they had already done something similar in the previous films. Also, the Lincoln Lawyer is here!



[Review on Letterboxd](https://letterboxd.com/hungryroy/film/jurassic-world-rebirth/)

---

#### Supergirl (2026)

Watched on 2026-06-24: ★★★½



{{% photos supergirl-2026 %}}

 I really wanted to love this one, but it just didn't come together that well. It's still a good and perfectly watchable film, I just expected more and was disappointed. The needle drops felt out of place, and the payoffs in the relationship between Kara and Ruthye feel a bit unearned. I do think Milly was a good Kara, and Momoa's Lobo was pitch-perfect, though I'm not sure he needed to be in this film.

I re-read Woman of Tomorrow after the film and while I mostly understand why they made the changes they made, they really did lose so much of the essence of the source material. And I wish they had at least done that magic bubble thing that Krem does on Supergirl, though having Comet might have been too much.

Despite everything, I think the film did a decent job setting up Kara for further action in the new DCU



[Review on Letterboxd](https://letterboxd.com/hungryroy/film/supergirl-2026/)

### TV

#### Daredevil Born Again S2

Watched on 2026-05-06: ★★★★½

I was a bit down on S1 because it felt like an incomplete story, and throughout S2 I was iffy about the pacing and as the final few episodes approached there was still so much left unresolved. But the last 2 episodes just did so much and was very satisfying and the season had an excellent finale which absolutely stuck the landing and saved the season.

Three out of the existing five Daredevil seasons so far have focused on Fisk, so I'm happy to move on from him and hoping for and looking forward to a much more brutal S3 for this one!

---

#### Pop Culture Jeopardy

Watched on 2026-06-05: ★★★★

This was a fun surprise drop and an excellent second screen watch, although I found Colin Jost a bit dry (but about the same as he is on SNL, so I guess that's fine). And you just thought that one team would run away with the whole season but they managed to put up a fight and have some excellent twists in there!

---

#### Spider-Noir

Watched on 2026-06-05: ★★★★★

This was so good, especially Nicholas Cage's eerie portrayals of when he is unable to control "the spider". The whole setting and atmosphere and costuming and storyline and the adaptations of the different villains were great and I'm amazed they managed to get the fantastic Brendan Gleeson chewing the fat as Silvermane! I watched all but one episode in B&W and it was good, but I probably wouldn't mind rewatching the full color version as well!
