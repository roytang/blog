### My Week

- Last Monday evening, my brother was taking Nephew #1 and Nephew #2 to attend a judo training thing at UP Vanguard. I decided to come along to (a) watch; and (b)walk around the old university a bit, as it's been a whole pandemic ago [since I've been there](/2020/01/saturday-morning-up/). Changes in the local jeepney route have made it much harder to just go there on a whim! Anyway, I did enjoy watching the (very young) nephews do their judo thing, but I was mostly there for the walking. It's always interesting to see how things have changed on campus, all the new buildings etc, but the main attraction for this visit was the new mall-like shopping center that they had built after [the old one burned down back in 2018](https://www.abs-cbn.com/news/03/08/18/another-up-landmark-destroyed-fire-hits-up-shopping-center). I think this new SC only opened last year, and it is huge compared to the old one, pictures below. It's basically a 3-story minimall, with food outlets like Shakey's and Dunkin, a PowerMac store, a bank, an arcade, and other stuff. There is a tiangge area on the ground floor and a more budget-friendly food court on the second floor, and the old photocopying and printing services seem to have been relegated to the sparsely-populated third floor. It feels much more upscale and commercialized compared to the old SC (something for which the university has been subject to [criticism](https://www.rappler.com/philippines/up-alliance-stands-against-dilimall-university-commercialization/) and [lawsuits](https://newsinfo.inquirer.net/2155414/up-officials-2-firms-face-raps-over-mall-food-hub)). While I understand the concerns and the sentiments, the new "Dilimall" at least feels much more spacious compared to the old SC, which always seemed crowded and underlit.{{% photos 20260309 %}}

### Word Power

I used to do [the NYT Spelling Bee regularly with the trivia team](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=phy_Noan24I) back in the day, but a while back our regularly-scheduled sessions dropped off. During the past few months I've started doing them again by myself. It's much harder without multiple voices in my head reminding me of words that I should already remember, so I've been using the hints a lot to hit those queen bees (I only use the hints if I've already hit genius). To help with my retention, I've decided to add a "Word Power" section here to note words/terms I've seen recently (not necessarily new to me, but words I should remember more easily) on the NYTSB:

- ANOXIA: Loss of oxygen (also ANOXIC)
- BIALY: A Polish bread roll
- CURACAO: A liqueur flavored with the dried peel of the sour orange (this one has some sort of ligature on the C, but the NYTSB accepts it)
- EPICENE: Having characteristics of both sexes
- LENTO: Slow tempo
- NENE: Hawaiian goose
- NEWEL: The post around which a circular stairwell revolves
- ORATORIO: A musical composition with dramatic text
- PELLICLE: A thing skin or film
- PULI: Hungarian sheepdog with a wooly coat
- RAITA: Indian yogurt side dish/condiment
- TENON: A thing that forms a joint with a [Mortise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mortise_and_tenon)
