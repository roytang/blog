I finished and completed Spider-Man 2 last night! This is the first game I managed to finish after borrowing a PS5! 

### Notes

- I generally enjoyed the game, which is not surprising given I enjoyed the first two games. 
- The mechanics of the game are about the same as the previous two games (web-swinging, combat challenges, quick-time events, some puzzles, etc) so I'll only note new things here.
- I did like how you get to play as both Peter and Miles. But it was kind of weird that whenever you switch, the new guy appears in roughly the same location as before. I get why this is so gameplay-wise, but I would have imagined each Spider-Man patrolling a different area of the city or whatever. I guess it's more like "Miles called Peter over to this spot where he was, then left."
- I like how they build-up Kraven into a really big city-level threat with a big army and everything. I also like how they adapted Harry and Venom for this universe. I was totally expecting Green Goblin action after Harry made his appearance, but I'm happy with what I got. (Also if I had looked at the skill tree ahead of time I would have been spoiled!). 
- Peter and MJ repeatedly make references to time they spent in Symkaria which I don't remember, apparently because I didn't play [the relevant DLC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marvel's_Spider-Man%3A_The_City_That_Never_Sleeps) and it was mentioned at the start of Miles Morales but I totally forgot.
- In this universe, New York has places like Avengers Tower and the Four Freedoms Plaza and even the Sanctum Sanctorum, but there's no mention of any other active superheroes, they're just easter eggs more or less.
- Those drone-chasing minigames ("Unidentified Target") were terrible, and for the harder ones I ended up having to turn on chase assist. (Props for the detailed accessibility settings though, not just a difficulty toggle.)
- They did not bring back the cel-shaded animation costume from the OG game! That was my favorite! At least Miles got a couple of Spider-Verse costumes though. There are a lot of costumes in this game I dislike though.
- There are a number of bugs I encountered along the way, such as enemies randomly getting stuck in T-pose in the middle of combat, enemies not loading at all, that sort of thing. They're not very common, I encountered such issues maybe less than 10 times through the run. (I heard the Steam port was much more problematic, so I'm glad I played on console.) ### PS5 Notes

Some PS5 specific notes, since this my first PS5 game:

- I am not a fan of these touchpad swipe inputs!
- the automatic screen captures on achievements now has this curved black overlay that covers like a fourth of the screenshot, so annoying.
- After I finished the game, I wanted to back up my saves (since this was not my PS5), and I found out only saves for PS4 games can be backed up to USB! For PS5 games the only option is to backup your saves to the cloud which requires Playstation Plus! So greedy Sony!

### Stats and Completion

Total playtime: roughly 31 hours. I played for a bit more than two weeks, roughly the same timeframe as the first two games.

I have 100% trophy completion! This is my first PS5 platinum!

{{% photos 20260306235541 %}}

Completion was fairly straightforward, you get most of them just finishing the main story and doing all the activities. There are some that are mostly just easter eggs about lore or New York City that I had to look them up.

### What's Next

IDK if we should be expecting a sequel to this any time soon, though they certainly left enough hints about what to expect. Insomniac is releasing a Wolverine game later this year instead, but again this isn't my PS5 so IDK when I'd actually get to play that one. Even if a sequel comes out, rumor has it Sony is cutting back on PC ports so who knows when I get to play that!

Recently I was looking through my old PS3 library though and I found out that we have a copy of Amazing Spider-Man 3 for PS3! My brother was the one to play through this and I never did; YT videos suggest it's a bit similar to these games though, maybe I'll give that a shot at some point.
