### Movies

I have not really been watching movies this year so far! There are a bunch of new-to-streaming ones from 2025 that I'm interested in, but sitting down to watch a movie requires a certain combination of mood, time, and energy that hasn't come by too often lately.

#### One Piece Film: Strong World (2009)

Watched on 2026-02-09: ★★★½



{{% photos one-piece-film-strong-world-2009 %}}

 I think this was my first time watching a One Piece film? Maybe second. I don't usually watch movie spin-offs since they feel like filler, but this was fine, all things considered. Animation was at least a step above the usual One Piece anime episodes. And Shiki the Lion had an interesting devil fruit ability too, though I find it laughable that he is referred to as a "legendary pirate" given the endgame-level powers that we see at the current-day point in the series!

### TV

#### The Good Doctor

7 seasons, finished watching on 2026-01-21: ★★★½

This was a series I have been watching on and off with my mom over the past few years. She likes the character drama and I like the medicine stuff. The show is decent and me and my mom mostly enjoyed it. The final season became available on Netflix recently so we decided to finally finish it. The lead character was played by Freddie Highmore from Charlie and the Chocolate Factory! The portrayal of autism can be a bit exaggerated (one of the scenes from this show where the lead character was poorly screaming "I AM A SURGEON" famously went viral a while back), but I found it okay. And in the final season Shaun mentors a new student doctor also on the spectrum and has difficulty with her; I thought it was a nice touch.

#### Fringe

5 seasons, finished watching on 2026-01-30: ★★★★½

Fringe was one of those sci-fi shows from the aughts that seemed interesting but I never really got into for whatever reason. I have notes that say I watched it briefly in the UK when I first sampled Netflix, but I have no recollection of that. When it popped up on Prime Video late last year I happily started regularly having it running on the second screen while I did other things.

Fringe starts off as a fairly standard monster-of-the-week procedural with hints of an ongoing mystery through the first season. What I like about the show is that they don't let their mysteries linger too long - a problem I had with shows like the X-Files (another one I want to binge through someday). At the end of each season they usually answer many questions and resolve a lot of plot points, although this often leads to new questions and new plot points because hey, you gotta set something up right? 

After the first season, the show's mythology develops quickly, evolving from the MOTW format to wider story arcs involving parallel dimensions, time travel, mysterious beings, etc. The lead characters Peter (played by Casey from Dawsons Creek) and Olivia (played by Anna Torv who I've never seen anywhere else) develop a strong will-they-wont-they relationship and honestly most of the end of season twists seem focused around giving excuses for them not to be together, but they get there eventually. Also Peter's father is portrayed by Denethor from the LOTR films! The sci-fi is mostly excellent, and a classic sci-fi icon even appears in a recurring role. The show takes a drastic turn for the truncated fifth season, but I think they still manage to sew up all the narrative threads and lingering questions from earlier seasons. The series finale wasn't as splashy as I'd have liked, but I guess it's not really that kind of show.

#### Agatha Christie's Seven Dials

Finished watching on 2026-02-03: ★★★

Was looking forward to this, because it was from one of her books I hadn't read yet, but ended up a bit disappointed because it was nothing like the sort of Agathe Christie story that I have come to expect. It plays out more like a regular detective thriller than a whodunnit really. Good performances though.

#### The Lincoln Lawyer

4 seasons, finished watching on 2026-02-08: ★★★★

I am a sucker for a good lawyer show and I binged through the first 3 seasons of this one a couple of years ago so I happily burned through season 4 as well when it dropped earlier this year. I enjoyed it so much I also recommended it to my parents because it felt like the sort of thing they'd enjoy and my mom finished it already and my dad is almost done, so recommendation success! I like most of Mickey Haller's supporting cast too. Lorna is played by Quinn from HIMYM!

I never wrote about the first 3 seasons, but I did get a chance to remember most of the plots because the parents were watching:

- season 1: Tommy Elliot case. A decent starting season and intro to the character. Rebecca from The Practice appears here as a judge!
- season 2: Lisa Trammel case. Some excellent twists and turns and a shock finish. I think this was the best season so far.
- season 3: Julian La Cosse case / Glory Days murder trial. Another good season which finds them butting heads with corrupt LEO.
- season 4: Sam Spades murder case / self-defense. Mickey Haller experiences the brutality of prison first-hand and even has a surprising ally on his side for the defense. I rate this season a bit lower because I did not like how the trial ended.

#### Royal Rumble

1988, 1989: ★★★★

A friend told me the new Royal Rumble was on NF, but when I checked it out I found that they had all the old ones starting from the very first rumble in 1988! So I ended up watching that one instead, and the second one as well. This was basically my childhood so it was a fresh blast of nostalgia and good fun honestly. 

- 1988: This first one only had 20 entrants! And Earl Hebner was already a referee back then! Jim Duggan won this one.
- 1989: They have 30 entrants this time. I enjoyed DiBiase being a sleazeball trying to money his way through this one, and the Jake the Snake antics that gave them a clear reason why Andre the Giant didn't just steamroll through the main event. Big John Studd won this one.
