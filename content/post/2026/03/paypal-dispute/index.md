title: Paypal can charge you a 'Dispute Fee' even if you did nothing in a transaction

On the 15th of January I got the following email from a stranger:

> Good day Roy, 
> 
> I just sent money on your PayPal by mistake. 
> 
> Accidently I typed the wrong email and sent you money that was supposed to be sent to another person.The amount is $345 USD
> 
> I kindly ask you to send the money back on my PayPal account please. 
> 
> My email address is [REDACTED]
> 
> I already contact PayPal customer service, however, they couldn't action anything from their side because the transaction was completed. 
> 
> If I don't get a reply from you, I will action a dispute through my bank to get a refund. 
> 
> Therefore, again, I ask kindly to avoid disputes and get my money back please 
> 
> Thank you
> 
> Kind regards

Now, when you're wary about everything, something like this easily feels like it's probably a scam. But when I checked my Paypal account, money DID come in:

{{< photos paypal-received >}}

And IIRC there was also a "REFUND" button there somewhere, so in theory I could have just done that. But again, I'm wary of scams and I was afraid this might come back to me in some way because I didn't understand how Paypal works. If this was the real deal, the lady would still get her money back when she filed the dispute, so I thought that would be fine so I just left it alone.

I seldom use my Paypal account (though I have some annual subs using it), so I didn't think much of it and just left it alone for a while. After a couple of weeks, Feb 2nd I got this email:

> Subject: An update on your PayPal Case PP-R-PLS-614540602
> 
> Case update
> 
> Thanks for your patience during our review of your bank reversal case. Unfortunately, we're unable to reimburse you the reversed payment because this transaction isn't eligible for PayPal Seller Protection for the following reason: Other.
> 
> This case is now closed.
> 
> Case detail
> 
> [DETAILS REDACTED]
> 
> We have charged you a Dispute Fee of $8.00 USD, since your account was categorized into Standard Dispute Fee tier. For more details about the Dispute Fee, please see our User Agreement.
> 

And I was like WTH! And so I checked my Paypal account and sure enough, the amount originally sent to me in error was refunded and on top of that I was charged an $8 "Dispute Fee", which is ridiculous. I had nothing to do with this transaction, did not participate in it or whatever. If I wasn't the type of person who never misses an email I might have never known about it until logging in to my PayPal account!

I started a convo with Paypal support and they basically told me there is nothing they can do:

> Support person: This is an external chargeback dispute, which is why you were charged a dispute fee of $8.00. Unfortunately, we are unable to reverse this fee.
> 
> Me: it doesnt seem fair that even though i had entirely no knowledge of this transaction and this dispute i am charged this amount!
> 
> Me: i found an email from last month from the other party saying they had sent this amount to my account in error. apparently i had not responded in time so they did a chargeback
> 
> Me: i was literally doing nothing and got charged for it
> 
> Me: is there nothing that can be done?
> 
> Me: i have never received payment as a seller via paypal before, so i dont have any history of this sort of thing
> 
> Me: this seems like a terrible way to run a service
> 
> Support person: This was an external chargeback filed by the sender, and the case is now closed.

Utterly unhelpful. I can understand that this is an external cost to Paypal and they need to charge the dispute fee, but why would it default to the person who did nothing to participate in the transaction? The sender was the one who sent the money by accident, so unless she provides proof that she was scammed or whatever the dispute fee should default to her! 

(*I didn't think of that last part when talking to support before, I sent them a new message just now asking if the fee can be charged to the sender instead, but I am not hopeful.*)

Since Paypal support was unhelpful, I tried sending an email to the original sender, asking if she can reimburse me for the dispute fee since it was her fault anyway. I did not have high hopes that this would work, but it was worth a shot. That was a month ago; I didn't want to write about this until she had a chance to respond to be fair, but probably one month was more than enough.

Up to this moment my Paypal account is still sitting at a negative $8 balance. I am unsure what to do with it. I will probably just leave it negative for now and see what happens the next time I need to use Paypal.

I know that $8 isn't that big an amount in the grand scheme of things so in theory I could just eat the cost, but I don't want to accept that unfairness. It just feels ridiculous that I got charged a fee when I did nothing at all. Also, I don't have a regular source of income so even small unnecessary expenses like this are annoying! Again, a terrible way to run a service.
