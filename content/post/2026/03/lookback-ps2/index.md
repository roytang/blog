Last weekend we were cleaning out some boxes from the house because of some renovations, and my dad found a big box marked "PS2". I was excited to open it, and it and inside (as expected) was a whole bunch of (pirated) PlayStation 2 games:

{{% photos 20260315_132148.jpg %}}

We also found a second smaller box with even more games (more pictures below) plus some Rurouni Kenshin OVAs on Betamax (?).

Notes:

- We are Asian, so the first thing my dad asked was "Can we sell these?" and I basically told him these had no value because they were pirated games. I [wrote a bit about that era of game piracy 21 years ago](/2005/02/some-thoughts-on-piracy/). At the time, we would basically be buying these games for the equivalent of 2usd per disc, which seems insane now. Stores selling PS2s would basically only be selling modded ones because that was just how things were done at the time. I mentioned this in the linked post above, but games were so cheap, we thought nothing about buying mutiple copies until we found one that worked with our finnicky PS2. The three copies of Star Ocean: Til The End of Time mentioned in the above post were in this box!
- I also have [a post from 2005 where I estimate that we had bought around a hundred pirates games](/2005/08/a-hundred-pirated-games/). I count 102 from these pictures, so it was an pretty good estimate! I took pictures of all the box contents mainly for the memories, which is the real value I got from this find. The photos are attached at the end of this post.
- A friend claims these pirated games have "Dvd Game 2" on the spine instead of the Playstation branding to avoid anti-piracy liability. I have no idea if that's true.
- We [bought the PS2 towards the tail end of 2002](/2002/12/31-dec-2002-happy-new-year-whatever/), right before I started working. We bought a PS3 in 2008, so presumably we had it for 6 years, though I think that the reduction of my free time due to work combined with the unreliability of our unit made it so I didn't really play as many PS2 games as I did the original PlayStation. The Nintendo DS also came out around 2006 and that started eating up more of my gaming time since I can play it at work.
- We actually found what I think is the old PS2 in the house [last year](/2025/04/1jy0bfh/) but I still haven't gotten it working.
- I tried to go through the games to find what games I actually "finished" at the time, doesn't seem like a lot. I was already blogging at the time, so the list below links to the reviews where available:
    - Final Fantasy X: Pretty sure I finished this at my friend David's house, with my regular gaming group watching and commentating like it was a spectator sport. No review, but [I finished this one in Jul 2002](/2002/07/110545308680470609/), even before we bought a PS2!
    - Final Fantasy X-2 ([First impressions from 2003](/2003/11/final-fantasy-x-2-first-impressions/))
    - Final Fantasy XII ([Review from 2007](/2007/01/final-fantasy-xii/))
    - Kingdom Hearts: I believe I also played through this at my friend David's. In fact, I may have done it in Proud Mode? But didn't beat Sephiroth probably.
    - Suikoden III
    - Suikoden IV ([Review from 2005](/2005/04/games-suikoden-iv/))
    - Suikoden V ([Review from 2006](/2006/05/suikoden-v-review/)): I'm amused that past me really said "Suikoden V is hands-down the best game in the Suikoden series" but I remember very little of the game today; Suiko2 remains the most memorable game of this series for me, but that may have been a product of much more free time.
    - Star Ocean Til the End of Time ([Review from 2005](/2005/01/games-star-ocean-till-the-end-of-time/))
    - Naruto: Narutimate Hero ([Review from 2004](/2004/06/games-naruto-narutimate-hero/)): This one is NOT among the games from the box, but I did have a review for it so I listed it here.
- That's 9 games total over the 6 year lifespan! By comparison, a quick skim tells me I finished at least 17 games on PS3 over a similar timespan. (That is a much more involved blog post for the future!)
- Aside from finishing a bunch of RPGs, I also played a number of fighting games and other stuff, notably:
    - Capcom vs SNK 2: Probably my most-played PS2 game of all-time. I played this a lot with the regular friend group and also at home with the brothers. I talk about it a bit in [my post about fighting games](/2019/08/fighting-games/). I loved this game enough to repurchase it as a PS2 classic on the PS3, though we never ended up playing that version much.
    - Timesplitters III ([Review from 2005](/2005/04/games-timesplitters-iii/)): I honestly don't remember this one a lot, but I guess I enjoyed it enough to write a review about it!
- Among the games found in the box, some that I notably did NOT finish:
    - Dragon Quest VIII: See [Games -- I Give Up On Dragon Quest VIII ](/2005/12/games-i-give-up-on-dragon-quest-viii/). I am not sure if I ever got back to it, seems unlikely.
    - Atelier Iris: I remember trying this one and not getting too far. Didn't really like the art style.
    - Kingdom Hearts II: I have no idea why I didn't play through this at the time. I ended up [finishing the game many years later on PS4 in 2017](/2017/10/924546017281785859/)
    - Dark Cloud II: I remember playing through a bit of this and being really impressed and liking it, but I believe I never finished it. Looks like it can be played on PS4 via a digital re-release though, which tempts me a bit lol.
    - Rogue Galaxy, Ys 6: I have zero recollection of these! Kind of wanna play these now though.
- Overall I look back on this era fondly, though this review made me realize I hadn't played the system as much as I thought.
- The boxes of games are still here, I kind of wanna go through the cases and disks just to double-check that there aren't any extra things of interest that snuck in there. Or maybe I'll just decide to dispose of them straight up. I am a bit tempted to keep the colorful game covers though, some have great art and I can't print those easily.
