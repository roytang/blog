### Limited Summary / Notes

- I like this set; it's big fun and not as aggressively fast as some recent other sets. I also had a reasonable win rate this time.
- They tried a new thing this set where there are 5 "primary" draft archetypes and 5 "secondary" ones. The primaries are easier to build around and meant to help newer draft players put something together. Each one focuses on one of the 5 primary tribes:
    - Kithkin (WG): Go-Wide Aggro
    - Merfolk (WU): A very fiddly archetype where you always want to be tapping your guys to trigger effects. I am not the biggest fan.
    - Elves (BG): Graveyard synergy. I tried this one early but never really got the hang of it and it's my worst performing archetype. It's not super aggressive and is more midrange, but it's great if you can get {{% card %}}Morcant's Eyes{{% /card %}} out early then you just trade guys and then late game make a bunch of elves. Either that or you win with a really big {{% card %}}Moon-Vigil Adherents{{% /card %}}. My experience with these is mostly losing to them!
    - Goblins (BR): My favorite of these 5 tribes; 2 of my 4 trophies were using these guys. It's an aggro deck, but with a lot of small sacrifice and blight and ping mechanics to give it some reach. Sometimes you get to put together a repeatable blight engine and then it's hard to stop.
    - Elementals (UR): You want to be casting spells with mana value 4 or more to trigger various abilities. It's fairly strong, if you manage to survive the early game. I never really got it to work very well.
- For the five "secondary" archetypes, they are harder to come together, but it can be done. My favorite of these is the WB Blight Midrange archetype, which is my highest win-rate archetype for the set. I only put together this deck twice, and both times I trophied! My favorite card in this limited format is the hybrid signpost for this archetype: {{% card %}}Reaping Willow{{% /card %}}. It conveniently also works in Goblins!
- Mechanics:
    - Changeling still works great as tribal glue across archetypes; I'm always happy to have any of the changelings be a midpack pick.
    - Blight: Really happy with the -1/-1 counter as a cost execution here.
    - Vivid: I didn't get to play the UG Vivid archetype much, but that's probably for the best. It tends to reward splashing and I **love** splashing and if I got into it I would probably be forcing 4- or 5- color too often and doing much more poorly.
    - Kindred: Not reallly a big deal, but every so often you're pleasantly surprised that a thing counts as a Kithkin or whatever.
    - Behold: Nice implementation of a fixed Champion mechanic.
    - Evoke, Persist, Convoke: Not really a big presence for any of these, but it's nice to see them again.
- Finally got the elusive 7-0 achievement!! (I have done this before, but this is the first time after achievements were implemented.)
- Mythic rank in limited remains elusive though. I got to Diamond during the last quick draft cycle towards the end of Feb, that was the best I could do.
