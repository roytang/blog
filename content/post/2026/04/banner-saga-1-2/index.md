The Banner Saga is a trilogy of turn-based tactical combat RPGs about a caravan of survivors trying to stay ahead of a dying world. I played through the first game back in 2016 and earlier this year I did a run of the second game. I do not yet own the 3rd game. The games aren't too long so I was originally planning to do a 2nd run of the 2nd game, but now I think that's not happening for a while, and it may be a while before I get around to buying and playing the 3rd game, so I thought I'd write about the first two games now.

### Story and Setting

The world of Banner Saga is based heavily on Norse Myth, a world where the sun has vanished and a giant serpent might be about to eat the world. You play the leader of a caravan of survivors, both humans and varl (giants), as they leave their lands beseiged by an ancient race of creatures called Dredge. The Dredge are a bit like a zombie plague, and something is driving them to swarm over populated lands, forcing people away from their homes. Along the way they have to fight through not only dredge, but also other humans and varl (because nothing brings out the worst of people than a crisis).

### Gameplay

Gameplay is split between:

- caravan management; making sure you have enough supplies and keeping people alive long enough to reach your destination
- story decisions; your decisions affect your caravan stats, whether new characters join your party, or whether some of them live or die
- turn-based combats; kills earn you experience and renown which can be used to level up  your characters and buy supplies for your caravan

All 3 modes feed into each other. If you do poorly in combat, your characters will need time to rest, eating up caravan supplies. If you make poor story decisions, you may lose combat characters or caravan supplies. Etc etc.

The turn-based tactics combat isn't super complicated; each unit has strength and armor stats and can either do normal attacks or use a special ability. Strength doubles as HP; if it drops to 0, the character is incapacitated. When a unit's strength drops, their attack power goes down as well, and if it's too low you need to chip away at their armor first before you can deal any real damage. There is also some magic, but not a lot. Healing is so minimal to be practically nonexistent, so battles tend to be a slugfest of trying to deal as much damage as you can before your own damage output gets too low. And you don't want your guys to get KO'ed because it means they're going to waste precious rest days healing them up.

The big twist in the combat system is how turns are taken. Each side gets alternating turns; one unit from your side gets a turn, then one unit from the enemy side gets a turn. So the less units there are on a side, the more turns they get. This continues until there is only one unit left on a side. When that happens, all the characters on each side take their turns in succession, so the winning side gets to gang up on the one remaining guy on the opposing side.

This combat system has a weird consequence. Normally in this kind of game, you focus fire on the enemies that are easiest to kill so as to take away enemy turns. But because of this game's systems, it becomes disadvantageous to pick out the weaker units, because it means the stronger enemies will get more turns. The optimal strategy becomes whittling down as many units as possible and keeping them alive but with low strength so that they aren't very effective. I always find this a bit infuriating because it goes against my every instinct! 

I'm not sure if it's because I find the combat system unintuitive or I am playing poorly, but I found these games very challenging. Story-wise, the outlook is very bleak (you are typically losing fighters and villagers left and right and just barely getting away from the dredge), and the combats have the same mood where it always feels like I just barely get through most fights.

### Runs / Completion / Stats

#### Banner Saga 1

- Playtime: 36 hours
- Achievements: 37/39

{{% photos bs1stats.png %}}

It looks like I did two runs of this game:

- started a run in 2014 after I first bought it, but didn't finish it then. I resumed in 2016 and managed to complete the game on Normal Difficulty.
- I did a second run in 2019, still on Normal, presumably to get a 2nd ending as prep for Banner Saga 2, which I bought around this time, but didn't get around to playing until 2026.

The story for this one was very tight and ended in a good spot. The main ending variants are whether a certain character lives or dies at the end, and this determines who leads the caravan at the start of the second game.

The two achievements I missed were for related to beating the game in hard mode. I already find Normal mode very difficult, so completion does not seem likely for any of these games!

#### Banner Saga 2

- Playtime: 8.5 hours
- Achievements: 26/55

{{% photos bs2stats.png %}}

The playtime listed above is for a single run! These games are not particularly long.

When you start a new game, you have a choice of loading a save from Banner Saga 1, and the choices you made in the save will affect your playthrough in Banner Saga 2. The save you load at the very least determines which character is leading your caravan when the story starts. (If you do NOT load a save, you just get to choose which of the two leaders to have.) A friend who played this trilogy ahead of me told me to make sure certain characters were still alive in my BS1 save, as they are quite useful here. (I didn't have to re-do any BS1 stuff, they were already still alive in my saves!)

I did my first run in Normal mode again with Alette leading the caravan. I was hoping to do a second run with the alternate choice, but maybe that's only going to happen later when I eventually buy the third game.

This game also adds a survival mode which I have not gotten into because it seems hard/annoying.

I was a bit more annoyed with the story in this game than the first one because the ending felt like much more of a cliffhanger.

### Summary and What's Next

Overall and despite my annoyance at the combat system, I would probably still recommend these games to any fan of the turn-based tactics RPG subgenre. The story is generally good despite being bleak, they don't take a lot of time, and are often dirt-cheap on Steam sales anyway. 

Eventually I will probably eventually buy and play through the 3rd game, if only to find out what happens in the story. The same friend tells me that in BS3, decisions from both BS1 and BS2 will have an impact, so I will probably also do another run of BS2 before then.
