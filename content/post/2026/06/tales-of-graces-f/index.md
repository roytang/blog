It is the year of our lord 2026 and here I am writing a review for a game I played on PS3! Quick explanation: I was tinkering around with my PS3 a couple of months ago and found that because I had re-upped my PS+, I had access to my cloud saves, and I still had saves from the early 2010s!! Nice! This led to me wanting to play through a bunch of games that I enjoyed and played through back then but never got the platinum for. During that time, I wasn't super obsessed either with writing reviews for every game I play, so re-playing through them now more than a decade later also gives me a chance to write about them! And the first one up was **Tales of Graces F**, which [I first finished back in 2013](/2013/12/2013-books-and-games/)!

Although Graces is the TWELFTH mainline title in the tales, that still feels insane to me because Graces is the FOURTH one I played. Before this, I last played Eternia on the PS1 (no. 3 in the series), so I skipped an entire console generations (which to be fair most of the in-between releases were either for consoles I didn't own or never got an English release).

### 2013 and Finishing the Game

Looks like I purchased Graces F on PS3 in January 2013:

I finished the main story (including the extra chapter 9 that came with the "F" version) by March, with around 53 hours playtime. ### 2026 and Completion

I was able to resume my save in April 2026! As would be expected, I spent a lot of time first durdling around and trying to remember how the game works. What made this challenging was that this New Game+ had "10x damage" enabled. It was necessary for a series of trophies themed around beating certain boss fights in 30 seconds, but it also meant I had to be careful in combat because enemies ALSO dealt 10x damage and if I wasn't careful I could easily get a game over even to normal encounters. I did enjoy playing through most of the story again, though of course I did end up skipping a lot of cut scenes since I already knew the gist of where it was all headed. 

I took so long to come back to this game that a new version has already been released for modern platforms: [Tales of Graces F Remastered](https://store.steampowered.com/app/2530980/Tales_of_Graces_f_Remastered/), but I prefered to play my old save from 2013!

It took completing this run + less than an hour in a third run to complete the trophy list, and I was done a couple of days ago. The in-game counter says my total play time came out to almost 150 hours, which meant I played around 100 hours over two months! That total playtime is typical for Tales games (though Arise was a bit more respectful of your time). HLTB has [the completionist time at around 135 hours](https://howlongtobeat.com/game/9588); I blame the 13 year hiatus for the extra time!

I tracked completion via [PSNProfiles](https://psnprofiles.com/trophies/1365-tales-of-graces-f/legendaryroy), which is convenient because I don't have to turn on my PS3 to check the list:

I got to achievement completion, but there's actually a lot more I could have done - mastering all the titles, 100% collection, etc - but I'm not that insane!

### Combat

While Eternia and the games before had a linear/2-dimensional battle system, jumping straight to Graces meant I leapt head-first into a new 3d battle system for the series. And it turns out to be really good jumping-in point too, as I consider Graces to be the start of what would evolve into modern Tales series 3d combats. They introduced a lot of good mobility options that made combats more interesting, and the A-artes/B-artes/CC system made for an interesting set of restrictions around which you can try to build your combos. I think all of the future Tales games built upon this solid foundation of 3d combat systems.

(By comparison, when I later got to play older titles like [Symphonia](/2021/12/symphonia/) and [Abyss](/2023/06/3ds-games/#tales-of-the-abyss), the then-nascent 3d combat systems felt very clunky in comparison.)

The story is very anime (typical for the Tales series) about a group of friends who made a pact during childhood, and later after a timeskip they are adults and one of them gets possessed by a powerful malevolent entity from another world. This is actually turns out to be among the less convoluted "two-worlds" Tales stories (a trope they go back to surprisingly often). The most notable thing here is that the main character is a nepo baby and grows up to be the lord of a town lol.

### Character breakdown

- Asbel: Typical thick-headed and often clueless anime swordsman protagonist, and on top of that he is a literal feudalistic nepo baby lol. He is a good main character to control in combat, and was the one I controlled most of the time.
- Hubert: Asbel's smartass brother who was traded away to another family for feudalism reasons. Probably my second-most-used character, tied with Sophie. He fights with dual blades and has excellent attack options but can be a bit of a glass cannon because of his recovery times.
- Sophie: A mysterious girl from another world (Tales of Eternia's Meredy would like a word). She is a fistfighter who also serves as secondary healer and I used her afair amount as well.
- Cheria: The party's main healer, Asbel's childhood friend and love interest (not that he shows much awareness of that for most of the game). I dislike her very frilly character design and had her in a different costume for most of the game. She uses throwing knives and she gets to cast Indignation in this game! Cheria and the previous 3 were my main party of choice for any challenging combats.
- Pascal: The quirky air-headed inventor who is without a doubt my favorite personality-wise in the crew. Unfortunately she doesn't handle very well in combat with her charged spells and ranged combat with her shotstaff making her very vulnerable up close.
- Malik: Token "old guy" in his 30s lol. Malik uses bladearangs for both melee and ranged attacks, but I found his A-Artes combos a bit hard to use and chain properly, and he ended up as my least used character.

### Graphics

While I do think the anime-style graphics of the game are decent enough for a PS3 game of its time, it is a port from the less powerful Wii platform, so it's a bit more low-poly compared to the first real PS3 release, Tales of Xillia.

### Other Gameplay Elements

#### So Many Titles

One thing I want to note about this game is that I think this is the Tales game with the most number of available titles for each character. There are soooo many! There are two titles for each arte, depending on arte usage!! And you want to use them all because leveling them gives stat boosts and unlocks new artes and costumes and so on. Probably the most involved titles system in the series; I love it and I hope they do this again in the future.

#### Dumb Minigames

Dumb minigames are of course a staple of JRPGs. There aren't a lot here, and most aren't too bad. The worst one is the card game *Magic Carta* where you have to match quotes from previous Tales games with the character who said them. Not only was this very challenging because there was time pressure and it's hard to remember quotes from all of these games, but also because there are soooo many characters, and even worse, around half of them were from games I've never played!

#### Cameo Battles

A staple of the Tales series that I've come to enjoy as I play most of them - you get bonus battles against characters from previous games! Unfortunately in Graces you get 3 cameo characters, only 1 of whom I actually knew from another game - Kohaku from Tales of Hearts and I didn't even recognize her at first because her name was localized differently.
