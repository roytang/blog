### Squaresoft

Square's output during this period was insane. Half of this list (11/22) are their RPGs released from 1997 to 2000, and honestly each one was a banger. I think modern Square-Enix is also pretty good in terms of number of releases, but many of them are remakes (some from this list!) and very few are hits. It feels insane that they released FF7-9 AND Tactics all within this 4 year period!

#### Final Fantasy VII (1997)

Rating: ★★★★★

I believe I watched a friend playing this one during summer vacation, and he named Barret something like "Summer97". This was probably before we even had a playstation of our own! I suspect we got our own playstation later that year during the holidays maybe, and FF7 was one of the first games we got, mostly because I really enjoyed watching my friend play. This was the second FF game I had played, after FF3/6 on the SNES, which I'll talk about if I ever get around to writing an SNES games post. I enjoyed FF6 but didn't have a lot of time with it; with FF7 I had way more time with the game and played it a lot. I'm pretty sure I did almost everything the OG game had to offer - raising Gold Chocobos, Knights of the Round, beating the two superboss weapons etc. The minigames were also much less annoying than the remake! I'm looking forward to maybe doing all of these in [Final Fantasy VII Revelation which was just announced](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8JszLth0_Gc)! 

Despite all that, I don't believe I have ever replayed this game after the PS1 era. (Maybe I should! It is on Steam!)

#### Final Fantasy VIII (1999)

Rating: ★★★★

This was kind of the "black sheep" of the PS1 trilogy of FF games for me, but I still enjoyed it, grumpy emo protagonist and all. I liked the whole time loop aspect and the Laguna flashbacks and of course Triple Triad was great.

My favorite memory of this game is that I never fully understood the junctioning system throughout most of what might have been my first and only run. While I knew I needed to junction spells to my stats, I did not know that I could junction a higher quantity to get a bigger stat boost! I went into the FINAL DUNGEON only having like 1 Ultima junctioned to each stat. When I got to the final boss for the first time I hit a brick wall and it was so hard and I didn't understand why until I learned that my junctioned stats were way too low!

Like FF7, I have never replayed this game after the PS1 era.

#### Tales of Destiny (1997)

Rating: ★★★★★

This was the first Tales game I ever played! I enjoyed it a lot and that led to a lifelong love of the series. Some things I remember:

- the game had a random name generator function when naming the lead character (Stahn), except it literally just put random characters together so the result was often something unpronounceable
- favorite character: probably Rutee
- favorite attack: Stahn's Dragon Swarm ("Soryu ren gazan!!")
- there was a minigame where you had to answer a long series of multiple-choice trivia questions about Namco videogames, including some I'd never heard of at the time (c'mon, Tower of Druaga?), I did a lot of tries to beat it!
- the random encounter rate in this game was annoyingly high. However, later in the game you can get an item called "Tricycle" I think which completely turned off random encounters on the world map, but in exchange, your movement became ridiculously slow and I thought this was hilarious
