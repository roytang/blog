Trying to very slowly work through my Steam backlog (an impossible task) by playing through some shorter games in addition to my favored very long RPGs. First up is *Mighty Gunvolt*.

This 2014 game is an 8-bit shooter akin to the Megaman games featuring characters from three different indie game franchises: [Azure Striker Gunvolt](https://store.steampowered.com/app/388800/Azure_Striker_Gunvolt/), [Mighty no. 9](https://store.steampowered.com/app/314710/Mighty_No_9/), and [Gal*Gun](https://store.steampowered.com/app/851890/GalGun_2/). 

It was a bit weird that this game was even in my Steam library, as I have not played any of the above franchises at all. I couldn't trace it to any Steam purchase or Humble Bundle or whatever so it was a mystery. Steam history told me I added it as a code in 2015, but where did I get it from? Finally figured out that a friend who didn't have Steam got it for free somehow and gave me the code and of course I just always accept this sort of thing. And I finally played through it 11 years later!

The game itself is very straightforward and short; there are 3 playable characters, one from each of the franchises mentioned above and each with slightly different movesets. But at it's core it's a jump and shoot thing like the 8-bit Megaman games. There are only 8 stages to play, each with a boss fight, and a final boss-only stage. Four of the stages are optional. Each stage is a straightforward "walk to the right and get to the boss" kind of thing and they are generally unremarkable and not very difficult. The boss fights were kinda good though; each one took me at least a few tries to figure out the attack patterns.

The "lore" (such as it is), is presented in what appears to be poorly-translated walls of text. I have a sample attached to this post. These are so bad I can only assume they are an intentional parody of poorly-translated games of the 8-bit era because otherwise WTH.

The game is very short; I completed all 12 achievements in under 5 hours. The most difficult achievements were beating all 9 bosses (the Bio Lab was the difficult one for me) and beating a boss without taking damage (took me around 20 tries with the School boss fight).

It's not a particularly good game, but at least the boss fights were entertaining. I'm glad I did not pay money for it. 

Rating: ★★★ 3/5 stars.
