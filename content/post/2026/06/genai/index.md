I wanted to have a post that I could point to when people ask why I don't use or like GenAI/LLMs. I had to restart this one a few times because it kept growing too long and full of links and I don't need that because many many people have already written many words about all the problems with GenAI.

(Note: I dislike how the term "artifical intelligence" has been completely co-opted as a marketing term by these big companies. The field of artifical intelligence existed decades before the concept of large language models and generative AI. I always take the time to use the term "GenAI" were possible to be more precise.)

The major problems for me (from most egregious to least) are:

- **Resource Usage**. There's the energy and water consumption, further straining our already struggling planet. Trillions spent on investments and data centers that could have been used solving problems like poverty or inequality. The rush for data centers drives up the prices of RAM and hard drives, making consumer electronics much more expensive (personally annoying for me). Then there's the effect om the internet commons: flooding them with slop. Scraper bots are constantly trawling the internet, hoovering up data without permission and straining servers, making it [more difficult to run sites like wikis](https://weirdgloop.org/blog/clankers) and forcing websites to block crawlers, even from the Internet Archive, threatening [historical records](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2026/03/blocking-internet-archive-wont-stop-ai-it-will-erase-webs-historical-record). [Filtering them is a challenge](https://www.jwz.org/blog/2026/06/today-in-the-scraperpocalypse/). Open source projects are [overwhelmed with slop pull requests](https://thenewstack.io/ai-slop-open-source/). Some projects like rsync have subscribed to "vibe coding" fixes with assistants like Claude. 
- **Dependence on Big Tech**. A couple of months ago someone was telling me about how they were using Claude in their work, and I asked him if he wasn't worried about their dependence on these big AI companies and how they are not profitable and heavily subsidized by VC investments and that they will eventually have to start raising pricing to turn a profit. (It's [already](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/30/what-a-joke-github-copilots-new-token-based-billing-spurs-consternation-among-devs/) [started](https://gizmodo.com/anthropic-is-jacking-up-the-price-for-power-users-amid-complaints-its-model-is-getting-worse-2000746923).) He didn't know about any of that. I think it's a big mistake for any industry to create a big dependence on software running on someone else's servers in order to do your work. **And** if this sort of technology is really important to the future it's a big mistake to centralize control of it in a few big tech companies. Also, when that investment bubble crashes, it's gonna be big.
- **Use of GenAI makes people dumber**. There have been [many](https://theconversation.com/is-ai-making-us-stupider-maybe-according-to-one-of-the-worlds-biggest-ai-companies-249586) [studies](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/the-art-of-critical-thinking/202507/is-ai-making-us-stupider-this-study-certainly-thinks-so), too many to cite. [Software Developers Say AI Is Rotting Their Brains](https://developers.slashdot.org/story/26/05/13/1949225/software-developers-say-ai-is-rotting-their-brains). A dependence on GenAI chatbots may also make learning more challenging for students, challenging traditional education models. Certain people may also be vulnerable to [AI psychosis](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/urban-survival/202507/the-emerging-problem-of-ai-psychosis) through excessive use of chatbots.

There are some other problems: fake news/propaganda, deepfakes, plagiarism, copyright issues, etc, but I think the above are the more important problems and are specific to this new technology.

I will admit that when GenAI initially arrived on the scene, I [tried it out a bit](/2022/09/ai-art-code/) but I quickly lost interest as it didn't seem particularly useful or helpful at the time. More like a toy or a novelty than anything else really. And as the problems emerged and the hype bubble grew, I just grew less and less interested.

Recently I've been thinking about starting to look for work again as a software developer and started poking around and talking to some friends asking about how they're affected by GenAI in their work and many of the convos and stories coming out are frankly a bit depressing. I have little to no interest in using AI assistants like Claude or whatever, and just reviewing GenAI-generated code or "managing agents" is not really the sort of work I am interested in doing. I do acknowledge that there are *maybe* some productivity gains to be had, with serious caveats, but without addressing the bigger concerns raised above I think it's all a nonstarter for me. I'm hoping that by outlining my thoughts on the matter I am better able to focus my efforts on finding work that would be more aligned with my own positions.
