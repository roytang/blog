#### Project Hail Mary (2026)

Watched on 2026-03-27: ★★★★★



{{% photos project-hail-mary-2026 %}}

 I [just read the book last year](/2025/07/project-hail-mary/) and this is overall an excellent film and adaptation. There's a bunch of changes in the source material, mostly to streamline things to make it more palatable as a movie. Notable changes for me were (a) shortened portrayal of Grace figuring out a lot of things (the runtime was already 2.5 hours, it might not have been tolerable to extend all those scenes); and (b) Stratt being portrayed as much more likeable in the film (in the book she was all cold and businesslike towards Grace).

I still wish they had found a way to leave Rocky out of the marketing because his reveal was one of the big moments for me in the book. But I can understand why they'd want to promote the film that way: their bromance is one of the best things about the film.

Will likely rewatch when this hits streaming!

#### The Imitation Game (2014)

Watched on 2026-04-13: ★★★★



{{% photos the-imitation-game-2014 %}}

 In my head, this film and Theory of Everything are twin movies that came out in the same year. The parallels are clearly there, but they have very different plots, cover different time periods and are biographies of two very different people. For one thing, Turing's accomplishments are largely overshadowed by the fact that they were classified and he was never celebrated in his lifetime and he died in ignominy by suicide due to the injustices of that time, making this film much more tragic. Cumberbatch gives a decent performance, and of course Keira Knightley is always delightful. And the film greatly expanded my knowledge of the Bletchley Park codebreaking efforts during WW1 (I confess to following a few wikipedia rabbit holes after watching the film).



[Review on Letterboxd](https://letterboxd.com/hungryroy/film/the-imitation-game/)

### TV

#### Wonder Man

Watched on 2026-03-06: ★★★★★

I found this one interesting because while it's about a person with superpowers, it has basically almost no superhero DNA at all. Dude just wants to live his life and practice his creativty and his chosen craft and not have to think about these powers at all. This is basically the sort of story that would almost never play out in a Marvel comic.

Also, what a journey Trevor Slattery has had in the MCU. Starting out largely as a joke/decoy villain in Iron Man 3 that did not at all justify having Ben Kingsley in the role, to his minor return in Shang-Chi, to this much more detailed and wonderful exploration of the character and his conflicts and his eventual redemption arc. 

---

#### One Piece S2

Watched on 2026-03-14: ★★★★★

This continues to be a fantastic adaptation, in multiple senses of the word. It's probably been almost two decades since I first read the Baroque Works/Alabasta arc in the manga, and it was a delight to go through these story arcs again. I was worried that the implementation of Chopper might turn out terrible, but they knocked it out of the park. Still the gold standard of anime adaptations. My only worry is that the anime is still so very long and there is so much stuff that happens later on that I would love to see adapted with this cast but that's gonna be soooo many seasons and who knows how long Netflix will let this wonder go on for?

#### Fallout S2

Watched on 2026-03-30: ★★★½

Kind of meh. It's not bad, but I've come to the realization that when it comes to Fallout, I don't really care that much what happened before the war. The worldbuilding in my head was largely set with the very first Fallout game, and further "shocking reveals" about Vault-Tecs intentions don't really change how I see them. Still fantastic set design and costuming though. I wish they would feature more of the environmental microstories like the games do.

#### Invincible S4

Watched on 2026-04-23: ★★★★½

Still largely staying faithful to the source material's plot lines. Having [just re-read the comic series last year](/2025/11/invincible/), it's fun seeing what little differences they've introduced. Notably, there is an entire hell episode exploring the roots of this universe's religous myths (and giving Mark some bad guys to hit to help him work through is own issues), and they gender-swapped Tech Jacket. Robert Kirkman has [an interview](https://www.ign.com/articles/invincible-creator-robert-kirkman-says-tech-jacket-was-gender-swapped-to-address-a-comic-book-shortcoming) where he explains how that last bit came about, and I thought it was wonderful:

> "One of the downsides of Invincible is Cory Walker and I were like 23 and 21 when we started that book, and your default when you’re not really paying attention is yourself," Kirkman said. "Every time we introduce another character, 'Oh look! It’s another white male! Who knew?' It’s just a shortcoming that we hadn’t quite noticed and done our due diligence to correct."
