Trying to go through my notes app today (I use [SimpleNote](https://app.simplenote.com/)) to clean out old notes, as I find I need to do occasionally.

I typically have many pinned notes that act as aggregators. As I go through the notes, if I find some links that fit into one of these buckets I will move the link to the specific aggregator notes and delete the old note if it was empty. The current pinned aggregators are:

- Blogs (blogs I want to check out for whatever reason). In theory I should just add them to my feed reader immediately, and sometimes I do (In fact, I added a few during today's pass. I can always remove them later). I think the ones that get aggregated into this note are ones that seem to have an interesting "rest of the website", either for design or feature or content reasons, that I might want to check out later outside of just their blog content. Or maybe the ones that I'm not sure yet if I want to follow, but there's an archive I wanna try checking out.
- Tools (tools I want to try)
- Music (music recos I wanna try)
- Books (book recos I might wanna check out despite having a terrible reading completion rate the past few years)
- Blog inspiration (things I found on other blogs/websites that I might want to try to add to this site)
- Games (these are mostly web games. If something is on Steam and looks interesting I'd wishlist it instead)
- Movies (movies to see)
- Short Fiction (some short fiction I have read and enjoyed and am collecting into a list for eventual sharing at some point I guess)
)

Some day there needs to be a reckoning, where I go through all of these lists. This is something I tell myself, akin to "someday I will finish my Steam backlog".

Other pinned notes:

- Family references. This is contact numbers, addresses, account numbers (for transfering money to or whatever), parents insurance info, etc. There is probably a better/more secure way to store these. Like for example the contacts app on a phone!
- Personal medical logs. I am of an age where there are many medical things to be monitored. Right now I regularly log my blood pressure, weight, and poops. Poops!

Other aggregator notes that are not pinned:

- Calendar of upcoming Movie/TV releases. I remove stuff from this list as they are released
- Watch list. Things on various streaming services I want to watch. Many times they rotate out of the service before I get around to watching them.
- Hospital/Doctors info. A consequence of being old and also of taking care of several patients myself.
- Gaming Notes. Things I note about the games I am currently playing
- Archival work notes. Notes about all the things I am archiving, like missing stuff etc.
- Grocery lists.
- A birthday calendar for my friends now that I don't use Facebook regularly enough to have it remind me. There might be better place/app for these, like IDK A CALENDAR?!? But typing them into a note is easy.
- mysterious new words I encountered in the NYT Spelling Bee. I put these in the weeknotes every week!

A lot of notes are stuff I collect for blog posts I plan to write eventually. Some examples:

- GenAI-related links, because I want to eventually have a big blog post collecting all my GenAI thoughts
- Linux setup notes. I actually transfered a lot of these into [a blog post](/2026/01/return-to-linux/) already, but there's also some newer stuff. I am keeping this around because I will eventually have to migrate to a different Linux flavor now that [Ubuntu is diving into AI](https://indieweb.social/@roytang/116477759095075758).
- "Filipino English" things that annoy me. The list isn't long enough to share yet.
- Notes on being a caregiver. I started taking these down during a low period last year when I was stressed about my patients' problems.
- Notes on fascism. A blog post I don't actually want to have to write.

If I find some things that I am immediately able to read and end up wanting to share. I can immediately send them to the [linkblog](/linkblog/). You might see a few I shared today if you click through.

On mobile, I use the SimpleNote android app. On desktop I use the website. My most common usage on mobile is when I find a link that seems interesting and I wanna check it out later I will save it to a note. Usually if there are many such links in a day, the note will become a list. I have many, many such notes. I sometimes also do this on desktop, but when browsing on desktop I am much more likely to just outright read longer articles and share them immediately if needed.

SimpleNote is functional, but it isn't perfect and has a number of issues. Off the top of my head:

- Sharing to Simplenote via the Android share dialog sometimes results in duplicate notes. I ended up just doing copy and paste instead of the more convenient dialog.
- Desktop website has a weird paste (Ctrl+V) behavior bug that I'm not sure if they've fixed already because I just set a workaround in my browser to avoid it.
- I wish it had an easier way for me to count the number of notes lol.
- Each note has a created/updated timestamps, but I have to click through a dialog to see it. Might be nice to have these visible at the top of the note.

At least SimpleNote is supposedly end-to-end encrypted so that's nice, and it exports to plain text files. I do think ideally I should be using something self-hosted that doesn't rely on someone else's server, but that seems like a lot of work that I'm not in the mood to do. (After all, I still have all these links to go through.)

I migrated to SimpleNote in late 2023. I previously used Google Keep mainly, but gave that up as part of a (still incomplete) de-googling effort. 

Before SimpleNote, I also tried [StandardNotes](https://standardnotes.com/) (I forget why I bounced off it), [Notion](https://www.notion.com/) (way too fiddly, and probably overkill for my use case. Also very limited in the number of notes a free account can use), [Trello](https://trello.com/) (more of a todo list than anything else), etc. Before Google Keep I was mainly using Evernote, which I abandoned when they started limiting the number of devices that free accounts can have linked to an account. In hindsight I should be glad that all of these platforms were very good at providing exports that I was able to carry forward to other platforms. The power of text files!
