Kev Quirk wrote a post about [who knows that you blog](https://kevquirk.com/who-knows-that-you-blog) (as a response to [Forking Mad](https://forkingmad.blog/who-knows-that-you-blog/) on the same topic). Kev writes:

> I'm similar to David and Alex - I'm not forthcoming with the fact that I have a blog, but I don't hide it either. I think that's mainly because most of my friends and family won't really care about what I write here, so it's a pointless exercise.

I think this is a truth that largely anyone who does any kind of [sincere blogging](/2021/02/sincere_blogging/) on the web will realize quickly enough: most people just won't care that much.

I am not shy about having a blog. I'm not big on self-promotion, but if someone asks IRL I will gladly give the link. And back when I was still active on Facebook where many of my IRL friends, colleagues and relatives are I would often share links to my blog posts. Some would commend me on my writing or comment on Facebook about the contents, but that is largely the scale of engagement I could expect. Almost nobody would bother to click through to other posts or browse the site or subscribe to RSS feeds or visit the site again unless I shared a link again. Sadly, "browsing the web" outside of their social media silos isn't really a thing of interest for most people like it is for many people who enjoy blogs. They won't bother seeking out or interacting with content that's not pushed into their algorithmic social media feeds.

I think I'm fine with that - I'm not really doing this to reach people who have no interest in this sort of thing, and most people I know IRL are in that majority. I always say I write primarily for myself, but I also know that the people who DO enjoy perusing blogs have their own ways outside of the algorithms to find blogs, and assume there is some (probably small) audience out there I am connecting with.

That being said, I do know some people IRL who follow this blog. Less than a handful probably, and I'm not sure how regularly they read. At least one person follows me regularly enough to DM me when I make any typos lol.

I do agree with Kev (in the post linked above) when he says that blogging sometimes suffers from connotations of ambition:

> I think some people tend to put "bloggers" in the same bucket as influencers, or podcasters. Which isn't the case - many of us bloggers have no aspirations of influencing anything, we just like to share out thoughts on a medium we control.

I do wish more people I knew IRL were into reading blogs and even writing their own. I sometimes try to convince people to start blogging. Like in some of my group chats there's always some people who'll post like long-ish reviews of thing and I would every so often suggest they start a blog so there's an archive of their reviews, but I always get a response like "they can't get into it" or they won't want to start from scratch with no audience. As if having an audience is the point! Oh well, it's not a hobby for everybody.
