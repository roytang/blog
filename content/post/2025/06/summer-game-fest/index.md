My last post felt a bit too serious, so I thought I'd write about videogames this time.

We don't have E3 anymore, but this past week was Summer Game Fest and that's kind of the same thing. Aside from the main SGF showcase, each of the big 3 console companies had their own showcases. I'm not big on buying new games anymore, but it's still fun to see what's up-and-coming. Here are the notable announcements for me, with YT links where appropriate.

### Sony State of Play

**Marvel's Wolverine** showed a bunch of gameplay. Nothing especially surprising here, but apparently it's not an open world thing, which makes sense I guess as he's not really a traversal hero like Spidey. I am unlikely to play this anytime soon, unless someone lends me a copy.

**Marvel Tokon: Fighting Souls** revealed some new characters. This game looks amazing, but the fact that it's a 4x4 game with a launch roster of a mere 20 characters isn't great. This one is multiplatform, and I do love fighting games despite being bad at them, so I may or may not play it eventually.

**God of War Laufey** was revealed. I haven't actually played through any of the GoW games, so I was surprised to learn that Deborah Ann Woll was in them **AND** that she was playing Laufey. My roughly-translated reaction in one of my Discords, with punctuation preserved:

> "Wait, her character is Laufey?"
>
> "The frost giant?!?"
> 
> "Father of Loki?!?"

This of course led to me getting spoiled about some GoW plot points lol. I'm (probably) not playing this though.

Not a lot of interesting stuff in this show for me. What I wished for but didn't happen: Sony revealing PS3 backward compatibility coming to PS5. As in I can pop in a disc from [my PS3 collection](https://roytang.net/2020/08/ps3-collection/) and just play it straight up, preferably loading my saves from cloud storage. It's a long shot, but a guy can dream. If they had announced this, I might have gone out to buy a PS5 immediately.

### Summer Game Fest Showcase

This show had a lot of announcements, but most of them weren't relevant to me. The only two items of interest for me came towards the end of the show:

**Street Fighter 6** revealed their Year 4 character lineup, which consisted of 3 new characters + a guest character - Tifa from FF7! Tifa wasn't the only interesting bit here, because one of the new characters revealed is Yasmine, who is from the PH! Represent! I've only been getting the annual releases when there's a deep enough sale, but a bit tempted to get this one early! [Trailer on Youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9dxDjF_dOXc)

**Final Fantasy VII Revelation** was revealed as expected as the highlight/show ender. It looks great and releases early next year, which is excellent timing for me as I am currently working my way through Rebirth. Honestly this reveal did a lot more to make me think about getting my own PS5 than anything in the Sony State of Play, though I might see how well the port does on my PC first. [Reveal trailer on Youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PH3ox5krci8).

### Xbox Showcase

I normally don't expect much from the XBox shows since they seldom have things of interest for me, but I knew from leaks that the following were coming:

**Persona 4 Revival** was revealed. This looks great and releases early next year, but seeing as how I haven't even gotten to either P5 Royal or P3 Reload, it's not likely I play this immediately. I think this had the best story among 3,4,5 though, so anyone who hasn't played it is in for a treat.[Pre-Order trailer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1osdiOAkBKc).

**Persona 6** was revealed via [a teaser trailer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CL-q0HgfMOY). This didn't really give us any real info though. And while I was excited to play P5 on launch, I'm still annoyed that P5 Royal came out just 4 years later with new content and I still haven't played through it, so I am hesitant to play this one on launch and will probably wait for the "final" version.

### Nintendo Direct

Nintendo brought the JRPGs! Ended up being the most interesting show for me, although I wasn't able to watch it all.

**One Piece Grand Gourmet** is not a game I am likely to play, but I am happy it exists! [Trailer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gaQqFfKj-SA)

**Fire Emblem: Fortune's Weave** looks great, but not enough to make me want to get a Switch 2. Though a friend pointed out that this one might be in the same universe as [Three Houses](/2021/01/fire-emblem-three-houses/), which was the last game I played in the franchise, that is a bit interesting. [Trailer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Rl5_C4sc5zk)

**Xenoblade Genesis** looks nice but it would be more interesting to me if I actually ever got around to playing through the copies of Xenoblade Chronices 1-3 I have sitting around here somewhere! [Trailer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=etNMdgFgDaE)

**Final Fantasy Resonance** was announced. Now this is interesting! A new turn-based HD-2D Final Fantasy title! It adapts the story of Brave Exvius, a mobile gacha game that I tried for a little bit but was discontinued last year I think. I have very little memory of it. This game adapts that story but the game itself is completely rebuilt, similar to the treatment for Octopath Traveller 0. Still unlikely for me to play this immediately, but again I am glad it exists. [Announcement trailer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wbsfQYI_IIY), [IGN Hands-On Preview](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Cet1Opcq0AA)

**Tales of Eternia Remastered** was already rumored to be coming out and barely got 3 seconds in Nintendo's sizzle reel, but the announcement made me happy. I loved the original back on the OG Playstation! I may not play it immediately because it's likely to be full-priced, but it's the one I am most likely to play first before the rest of these! A separate [announcement trailer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=n0Trr5yLK1A) was released outside of the Nintendo direct.

**Kingdom Hearts 4** got a [new trailer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=j151ek2rYs0), but I kind of checked out of this series after [playing through KH2](/2017/10/924546017281785859/). The gameplay is fun and the Disney stuff is nice, but the story and lore is just so damn obtuse! I may eventually play KH3 someday, and until then KH4 isn't really that interesting.

Nintendo topped off their show by announcing a remake of **The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time**. The [reveal trailer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r8eMoxo4ipE) did not show us a lot, but it's releasing later this year, so we should get more info soon. I only played through the game via [the 3DS remake](/2023/06/3ds-games/#the-legend-of-zelda-ocarina-of-time-3d) but didn't really enjoy it that much because I felt the camera control and some gameplay elements didn't age well; I'm hoping the remake fixes all of those issues. This is not quite enough to get me to buy a Switch 2 just yet, though I will admit to checking out the console prices right after. As a friend points out though, they are probably going to be using whatever engine they use here for the next mainline Zelda, in the same way that the [Link's Awakening remake](/2020/10/1322214646221205504/) led to E[choes of Wisdom](/2024/11/echoes-of-wisdom/); if and when this leads to a proper new Zelda title I am likely to buy in already.

### That's it!

I think this years reveals were fun and had more interesting things than previous years. For me, at least! I find it amusing how so many games are releasing in September to avoid GTA6, except for Nintendo not caring and just having Ocarina of Time go head-to-head with it! Anyway, we'll see how long I am able to resist buying any of these games!
