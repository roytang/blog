The very first thing you have to know about game development is that being successful is incredibly rare and incredibly hard. All those success stories you cite are lightning in a bottle; for every indie success story there will be hundreds if not thousands of failures and also-rans who lost money on the venture. If you must get into it, you have to be doing it because you love games and you find that you love the process of making games and you must be prepared to fail and maybe even fail repeatedly. It is an endeavor that go on for years possibly without meaningful feedback on whether you're on the right path or not. Being insane or already independently wealthy will help.

The second thing is that if you want to make games as a solo developer, you have to know that game development is a multi-disciplinary field. The major areas to hit are programming, art, music, and sadly marketing. You can outsource some of these, but that costs money and resources. You generally must also be very good at math, logic, problem solving, you have to be patient, focused, well disciplined, and willing to work hard. 

If none of that deters you, the first thing to do is to look up game programming tutorials, as that is the field that most people will not be familiar with (most people already know what art and music are and generally know how they work). I would recommend specifically looking for Godot tutorials. Probably watch a few tutorials first to get a general idea. This is a good idea even if you already have programming experience, as game programming can be a slightly different beast than what what you're used to.

It is a good idea to start small and not to aim to build some kind of MMO or big FPS game right off the bat as well. Build some small projects, get experience, developer your skills, ramp up slowly etc.

Disclaimer: Take the advice above with a grain of salt, from someone who's failed to get a successful solo gamedev project completed over the past couple of decades (barring a few game jam-level releases on itch)
